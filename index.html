Table Recommendations

Meals We Still Dream About

◆
Kyoto, Japan
Kaiseki Sashimi, Kyoto
Kaiseki · Omakase
Salmon and scallop over crushed ice, served as the opening course of a kaiseki dinner. The kind of precision that makes you put your phone down and just eat.
Tokyo, Japan
Tsukiji Outer Market
Market Breakfast · 7am
The freshest sushi standing at a counter before the city wakes up. No better start to a Tokyo morning.
Istanbul, Turkey
Sultanahmet Kebab House
Turkish Grill · Old City
Pistachio-crusted lamb chops grilled over charcoal, eaten on a terrace steps from the Blue Mosque.
Seoul, South Korea
Tosokchon Samgyetang
Korean · Since 1983
Ginseng chicken soup in a hanok village. A bowl that somehow tastes like history.
Busan, South Korea
Korean Fried Chicken
Street Food · Night Market
Double-fried, lacquered in a sauce that is simultaneously sweet, spicy and completely addictive. Eaten at midnight.
Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam
Pho Hoa Pasteur
Vietnamese · Since 1960
Beef broth simmered for twelve hours, served with a pile of fresh herbs at a table shared with strangers. Perfect.
Hanoi, Vietnam
Pizza 4P's
Japanese-Italian · Book Ahead
Japanese precision applied to a wood-fired Neapolitan base. The half-burrata, half-tomato pizza is one of the great surprises of Southeast Asia.
Bangkok, Thailand
Mango Sticky Rice
Street Dessert · April–June
Nam Dok Mai mango in peak season over warm glutinous rice, finished with salted coconut cream. Eaten on the street, standing up. Worth flying to Bangkok for.
Paris, France
Ladurée, Champs-Élysées
Pâtisserie · Since 1862
The macaron that all other macarons are trying to be. Buy a box, eat one on the pavement outside, immediately buy another box.
Naples, Italy
Da Michele Pizzeria
Trattoria · Since 1870
The original Margherita. No reservations, a long queue, two items on the menu. Absolutely worth every minute of the wait.

Who We Are

Two People From Bangalore, India.
One Shared Obsession.

We are Rohan and Ana, a couple who have travelled across 50+ countries over the last decade. We are not travel writers. We are not influencers. We are people who plan obsessively, eat everything, stay in places that make us feel something, and come home already thinking about the next trip.

Atlas & Archives started as a way to stop re-explaining our itineraries to friends. It became something we genuinely love building. Every recommendation on this site is one we've made with our own money, on our own time.

If you want to do a trip like the ones here, or somewhere else entirely, we'd love to help you plan it.
