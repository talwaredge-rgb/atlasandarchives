Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka
& Kawaguchiko

Tokyo  ·  Kyoto  ·  Osaka  ·  Kawaguchiko | Oct–Nov | Fly into Tokyo, Out of Osaka

Japan in autumn is not a destination, it is a feeling. But before the story, the structure: we fly open jaw, into Tokyo, out of Osaka (or the reverse). Open jaw fares are often the same price as a return ticket, rarely more than 5% extra. What you gain is everything: you move in one clean direction through the country and never backtrack. The logical flow works either direction, Tokyo to Osaka or Osaka to Tokyo. We added Kawaguchiko between Kyoto and Tokyo deliberately, to slow the pace before five days in the world's most relentless city.

Before You Go

Open jaw, not return. Fly into Tokyo (Narita or Haneda), out of Osaka (Kansai). Open jaw fares are usually identical to a return, occasionally 5% more. You move in one clean direction through the country and never backtrack.

No JR Pass needed. For this route you take the Shinkansen exactly once. A 14-day JR Pass costs around 50,000 yen per person. The single Shinkansen leg costs about 14,000 yen. Buy the individual ticket on the day or book via the Shinkansen app.

IC Card. Pick up a Suica or Pasmo card at the airport on arrival. Load 5,000 yen. Covers every metro, bus and convenience store purchase in Tokyo, Kyoto and Osaka. Top up at any station kiosk.

Pocket Wi-Fi or SIM. Reserve pocket Wi-Fi before you travel and collect at the airport. Google Maps in Japan is reliable but needs data constantly. Download offline maps as backup.

Where to Stay
Osaka · Kyoto · Tokyo
Osaka: Stay near the Midosuji subway line. Preference order: Namba (closest to Dotonburi and the airport train), Shinsaibashi (more upscale), Umeda (best for shopping). Kyoto: Near Kyoto Central Station, every attraction (Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama, Kinkakuji) is reachable from here. Being 10 minutes from the station versus 30 matters when you're going in and out all day. Tokyo: Shinjuku for first-timers, 7 metro lines, walking distance to Golden Gai and Gyoen. Asakusa for old-Tokyo atmosphere. Shibuya for nightlife. Tokyo Station if you plan day trips by bullet train.

First-Timer Notes
What Nobody Tells You
Japan has almost no English signage in the European sense, but almost everything has English text written somewhere underneath the Japanese. You'll figure it out faster than you expect. Where you genuinely can't, Google Translate's camera mode is magic: point your phone at any sign or menu and it translates in real time. You will not be lost. You will not go hungry.
Osaka CastleKuromon MarketShinsekaiTsutenkaku Tower
Dinner
Conveyor belt sushi for your first Japan meal, the experience is as fun as the food. Or Okonomiyaki, Osaka's savoury pancake. Note: it contains octopus (tako), chewy rather than meaty, no overpowering flavour. Most first-timers love it. Veg option: Gyozaoh or Green Earth on the strip. Finish with Rikuro's cheesecake, soft as a cloud.

Nara, Day Trip
Deer That Bow Back
Kintetsu train from Osaka Namba to Nara (40 mins, ¥720). Kofukuji Temple, Nara Deer Park, the deer genuinely bow before you feed them, and Todaiji Nandaimon, one of the largest wooden buildings in the world. Back in Osaka by afternoon: Umeda Sky Building 40th floor observatory (¥1,500) for city panoramas, then Tonbori River cruise at dusk.

Osaka
Castle, Shinsekai & the Best Street Food

4–5
Kyoto
Torii Gates, Golden Pavilion & Gion at Dusk
Train from Osaka to Kyoto Station (40 mins, ¥580). Stay near Kyoto Station, every attraction is reachable from here. Day 4: Fushimi Inari (JR Nara Line, 2 stops), go early afternoon, walk past the first ridge where crowds thin. Then Kinkakuji at late afternoon: the gold reflects in perfect stillness in the pond. Gion in the evening, Hanamikoji lane, kimono rental available (¥3,000–5,000 for 3 hours). Photograph the Grand Staircase at Kyoto Station at night, lit, beautiful, almost no one mentions it. Day 5: Arashiyama Bamboo Forest at 8am before tour groups arrive. Then ship your heavy luggage to your Tokyo hotel (takuhaibin, ask your hotel, ¥1,500/bag, delivered overnight). Travel light to Fuji.

Meals
Lunch stop in Mishima: Unagi Sakuraya for freshwater eel over rice, famous locally, worth the stop before the bus. Dinner non-veg: Houtou Fudou, flat noodles in miso broth with mountain vegetables, exactly right on a cold night. Dinner veg: Café Troisième Marché, small, French-Japanese, genuinely good. Why 2 nights here instead of a day trip from Tokyo: the highway into Tokyo is congested every evening. Two nights means Fuji at dawn, no luggage, no racing for the last train.
6–7
Kawaguchiko · Mt. Fuji
The Deliberate Pace Change
Shinkansen from Kyoto to Mishima (1 hour), then Fujikyu bus to Kawaguchiko (1.5 hours, ¥1,650). We added Fuji not just as a sight but as a pace change, Tokyo is exhilarating and relentless, and two quiet nights at a mountain lake resets you before five days in the city. Day 7: Fuji-Q Highland (Japan's most intense roller coasters, Fuji as backdrop), Panoramic Ropeway (¥1,000, views of both mountain and lake), Narusawa Ice Cave (¥350, a lava tube where temperature stays below freezing year-round, strange and beautiful). Swan Lake Cruise in late afternoon.Ryokan option: Kawaguchiko is the best place in Japan for a traditional ryokan, sleep on futon, bathe in an onsen, kaiseki dinner served in your room with Fuji as backdrop. ¥30,000–80,000 per couple per night including dinner and breakfast. Book 2–3 months ahead. If you do one ryokan night in Japan, do it here.
Souvenirs
Tokyo Banana (the city's iconic soft sponge cake, buy at Tokyo Station). Royce Chocolate (nama fresh chocolate, refrigerated, extraordinary). Kit Kat Japan, 40+ flavours including matcha, sake, wasabi. Proper matcha powder from Ippodo Tea in Kyoto. Washi tape and stationery from Itoya in Ginza (8 floors). 100-yen shops (Daiso, Seria), genuinely good design at ¥100.

Tokyo
Five Days in the World's Most Remarkable City
Highway bus from Kawaguchiko to Shinjuku (1.5 hours, ¥1,750). Your bags arrived at the hotel yesterday. Check in and give Tokyo the time it deserves.Day 8: Imperial Palace East Gardens (free, open Tue–Sun). Hibiya Park. Ginza for the architecture (Hermès building, Uniqlo flagship). Full day.Day 10: TeamLab Planets (book weeks ahead, ¥3,200), immersive digital art, genuinely unlike anything else. Toyosu Fish Market for context on Japanese food culture. Tokyo Skytree (¥2,100, 350m). Dinner: Florilege, French-Japanese tasting menu, Michelin starred, one of the ten best meals we have ever had. Book 2 months ahead.Day 11: Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building 45th floor (free views rivalling Skytree). Harajuku: Takeshita Street for the spectacle, Omotesando for the architecture. 14
Tokyo → Home
The Last Morning
Breakfast in the Tokyo Station basement (Gransta mall, some of the city's best grab-and-go). Narita Express (N'EX) from Shinjuku, 90 minutes, ¥3,070, runs every 30 minutes. Air India uses Terminal 2. As the city disappears below the clouds, you'll already be thinking about when you can come back.

The Case for Kawaguchiko

Most itineraries skip Kawaguchiko. We built two full days around it deliberately. After four days of Osaka and Kyoto the pace change is medicinal, and if Fuji clears at dawn from the lake it is the most quietly overwhelming sight in Japan.

The ryokans here serve kaiseki dinners, the onsens look out over the lake, and Fuji appears in the early morning when the weather cooperates. Coming in by Shinkansen to Mishima and then the Fujikyu bus keeps the journey itself interesting. Do not rush this leg.

Common Questions

Is the JR Pass worth it for this itinerary?
No. For a Tokyo to Osaka route with one Shinkansen leg the JR Pass does not pay for itself. The 14-day pass costs approximately 50,000 yen per person. The single Shinkansen leg you need costs around 14,000 yen. Buy individual tickets unless you are adding bullet train journeys beyond this itinerary.
When exactly is autumn foliage in Japan?
Mid-October to mid-November, peaking in early November in Kyoto and mid-November in Tokyo. Kawaguchiko peaks slightly earlier. The window per location is about 7 to 10 days. Check the Japan Foliage tracking app the week before you travel for real-time colour maps.
How many days do you actually need in Tokyo?
Five days is the minimum to cover the major neighbourhoods without rushing. Tokyo is a city of areas, each with its own character. Shibuya, Shinjuku, Asakusa, Yanaka, Harajuku, Akihabara and Shimokitazawa are all genuinely different. Five days lets you cover six of these properly with one day left for a day trip.
Is Japan expensive for Indian travellers?
Less than people expect, especially eating where locals eat. A bowl of ramen costs 900 to 1,200 yen. A standing sushi lunch is around 1,500 yen. A full kaiseki dinner starts at 15,000 yen. The mid-range sweet spot for a couple with good hotels and all meals out lands around 3.5 to 4.5 lakhs for 14 nights.
Do I need to speak Japanese?
No. Tokyo and Osaka are extremely easy to navigate in English. Google Translate camera mode handles menus instantly. Subway apps have English throughout. In rural areas communication is harder but pointing, bowing and a willingness to mime covers almost everything.
What should I absolutely eat in Japan?
In Osaka: takoyaki from a cart in Dotonbori, standing ramen at Ichiran, kushikatsu in Shinsekai. In Kyoto: tofu kaiseki, matcha everything, yudofu at a temple restaurant. At Kawaguchiko: the ryokan kaiseki dinner. In Tokyo: standing sushi at Tsukiji Outer Market, ramen at any neighbourhood shop with a queue outside it, tempura at Tsunahachi.
What to Pack - November

November ranges from warm in Osaka at 18C to cold at Kawaguchiko at 5C at night. Layer intelligently: a light down jacket, one warm mid-layer, base layers. Walking shoes are essential, Tokyo days average 20,000 steps. Cash matters: many rural restaurants, temples and small shops are cash only. Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs which reliably accept international cards.
